IN Realty

A locally focused, independent real estate brokerage servicing the Research Triangle Park area in North Carolina.

IN Realty builds positive experiences for buyers and sellers through proficient and unique personal service. It's knowledgeable brokers and in-house team of experts assists with all facets of real estate. IN Realty needed a brand that would support their ambitions and compete with the big box brokerages.

Logo

IN Realty's logotype is built on the bones of the typeface Brandon. The rooftop with arched window graphic substitutes "A" in the word "Realty" to visually at residential property.

Stationery & Collateral

The visual system has polish and personality: premium typography adds status, curated photography feels editorial and genuine, and ownable illustrative pattern work exudes energy and movement. The color palette consists of tonal blues that communicate growth, renewal, and energy.

Web Design

Once we established IN Realty's visual identity, we worked with their team to create high-fidelity mockups—complete with conceptual copywriting—to make the brand’s website come to life. A key design involved the main navigation and footer, key areas that not only create a cohesive experience for users, but also communicate the intentional and unity of a brand and its offerings.

Hinson's Printing

More than a print shop, Hinson's Printing is a family-owned and operated full-range operation.

Starting with a small offset press, a few finishing machines and only two employees, Hinson's has evolved its services to include wide-format, promotional products and direct-to-garment t-shirt printing. With 40 years under their belt they wanted to refresh their brand to go last at least another 40.

Logo Refresh

The original logo had become outdated and in need of a revamp. The job was to maintain the look and feel of the original, but revitalize it.

We started by updating the type to create a higher contrast and make the name visibly stronger. Lines were thickened to not lose strength at smaller scales. We also corrected the tic mark to an apostrophe for proper punctuation. The next step was to create variations for every use case.
